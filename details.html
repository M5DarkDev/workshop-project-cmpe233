4-Day Workshop Schedule

Each day is built to help you improve strength, mobility, nutrition, and recovery — step by step.

Day 1: Strength Training Fundamentals & Intro to Nutrition

Strength and conditioning are essential components for improving athletic performance. This includes a mix of compound lifts like squats, deadlifts, and bench presses, along with plyometrics and functional training. Progressive overload, proper form, and periodized training plans ensure consistent gains in strength and endurance. Conditioning drills such as sprints, HIIT, and agility ladders help develop cardiovascular health and explosive power, which are crucial in both sports and general fitness.

Morning Session

  • Full-body strength training focused on form and technique.
  • Muscle activation: core, glutes, back.
  • Coaching on compound lifts (squat, deadlift, press).

Afternoon Session

  • Lecture: How muscles grow + progressive overload explained.
  • Workshop: Build your personal strength routine.
  • Intro to nutrition: macros, calories, hydration tips.

Takeaways: Proper form, strength fundamentals, nutrition basics.

Day 2: Mobility Drills & Meal Prep Workshop

Nutrition fuels performance and recovery. Smart meal planning involves balancing macronutrients — proteins for muscle repair, carbohydrates for energy, and fats for hormone regulation. Timing is also key: pre-workout meals should focus on fast-digesting carbs and moderate protein, while post-workout meals prioritize protein and glycogen replenishment. Hydration, micronutrient intake, and meal prepping are also vital for consistency. Supplements like whey protein, creatine, and omega-3s can support specific goals if used responsibly.

Morning Session

  • Mobility flow to improve flexibility and range of motion.
  • Drills for hips, spine, shoulders.
  • Foam rolling and self-release techniques.

Afternoon Session

  • Live meal prep: make 2 performance-focused meals.
  • Discussion: pre/post-training meals.
  • Time-saving strategies for weekly meal planning.

Takeaways: Better mobility, smarter nutrition, meal prep mastery.

Day 3: Injury Prevention & Group Workout

Mobility work increases range of motion, enhances performance, and reduces injury risk. Drills like dynamic stretching, foam rolling, and resistance band exercises help activate muscles and improve joint health. Focusing on commonly tight areas (hips, hamstrings, shoulders) is crucial, especially for those doing strength training. Incorporating proper warm-up and cool-down routines, alongside stabilization exercises, keeps the body functional and less prone to strains or overuse injuries.

Morning Session

  • Understand common training injuries and how to prevent them.
  • Stability drills for knees, shoulders, and spine.
  • Functional exercises to strengthen weak points.

Afternoon Session

  • High-energy full group workout (strength + conditioning).
  • Coaching feedback on form and pacing.
  • Stretch and guided breathing cooldown.

Takeaways: Injury-proof your body, train with intensity, enjoy community vibes.

Day 4: Recovery, Q&A & Certificate Ceremony

Recovery is where real progress happens. Adequate sleep (7–9 hours for most athletes) supports muscle growth, hormonal balance, and cognitive function. Sleep hygiene — avoiding screens before bed, maintaining a cool room, and having a consistent sleep schedule — boosts sleep quality. Other recovery methods include active recovery, massage, stretching, and cold exposure (like ice baths). Tracking recovery with HRV or readiness scores can help adjust training load and prevent burnout.

Morning Session

  • Active recovery: light mobility and stretching.
  • Recovery tools: sleep, nutrition, cold/hot exposure.
  • Mindset reset: staying consistent long-term.

Afternoon Session

  • Open Q&A with all instructors.
  • Personalized feedback and guidance.
  • Certificate ceremony + group photo + snacks.

Takeaways: Final reflections, clear next steps, official certificate.
